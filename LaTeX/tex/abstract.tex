\section*{Abstract}
\label{sec:abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{\nameref{sec:abstract}}
The Algorithms and Data Structures 2 course at the Institute for Pervasive Computing has traditionally relied on static slides to teach concepts such as graph theory and algorithm visualization. While effective for conveying information, this format limits opportunities for students to engage interactively with the material and for teachers to demonstrate concepts dynamically.

Graph theory and algorithms are foundational topics in computer science, and their inherently dynamic nature makes them ideal for interactive exploration. To address this, this thesis presents a web-based application that enables both students and teachers to create graphs and simulate algorithms in a hands-on, intuitive way.

This document details the design, development, and testing of the application, focusing on its usability and features tailored to an educational setting. The result is a practical tool that enhances learning by allowing students to experiment with concepts and empowers teachers to demonstrate algorithms in a more engaging and flexible manner.
